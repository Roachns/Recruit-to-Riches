Project Scope

Recruit to Riches is a project analyzing individual high school football players recruiting rankings and how good of a probability indicator these rankings are on whether they will get drafted into the National Football League. Our team utilized the recruiting ranking website 247sports, which employs a proprietary algorithm that compiles prospect "rankings" and "ratings" listed in the public domain by the major media recruiting services. It converts average industry ranks and ratings into a linear composite index capping at 1.0000, which indicates a consensus No. 1 prospect across all services.

Exploring the Data

  • What towns and cities have the highest concentration of players rated in the 247sports composite?
  • Do recruiting rankings accurately reflect the skill of a player and their likelyhood to be drafted to the NFL?
  • What collegiate programs are highest rated recruits attending?
  • Do recruits that signed with certain collegiate programs have a higher chance of being drafted?

    • Recruit Origins

    Examination of the heat-map and stacked horizontal bar chart show us that recruits are prmiarily located in urban centers as opposed to rural areas. The highest concentration of recruits came from California, Georgia, Florida, Ohio and Texas.

    Star Power

    Of the hundreds of thousands of highschool football players only a limited amount are given star ratings by recruiting services. Of these , 1 and 2 stars make up 36%, 3 stars make up 52%, 4 stars make up 11%, and 5 stars make up just 1% of the total rated recruits. Each year only 7% of eligible players will be drafted. 5 and 4 stars dominate the early rounds 1 through 3 of the draft however 3 stars and 2 star rated players fill out the majority of the later rounds. No one star players were drafted during our analysis.

    Mascots Matter

    When it comes to your recruiting rank, players should be aware of which schools will put them in the best postion to be drafted. 5 stars have high chances of being drafted from Clemson while 4 stars should look to Iowa and Louisville. Interestingly 3 star recruits have good chances of standing out if they attend small programs such as Georgia Southern.
